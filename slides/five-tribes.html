Welcome to the Sultanate of Naqala

The old Sultan is dead. The throne sits empty.

In this fabled land of Arabian fantasy, you'll navigate a world of:

  • Ancient villages and bustling markets
  • Sacred oases and mysterious holy places
  • Five powerful tribes awaiting your command
  • Legendary Djinns with supernatural powers

Your goal: Maneuver the tribes, summon Djinns, and claim the Sultanate as your own.

Set the scene with the Arabian fantasy theme. The game immediately draws you in with its beautiful world-building. Naqala feels like a place from One Thousand and One Nights.

The Mancala Twist

Not Your Typical Worker Placement

  • Meeples start on the board — you move them, not place them
  • Pick up all meeples from a tile
  • Drop one-by-one along a path (Mancala-style)
  • Last meeple must match a color already on the destination
  • Collect matches — activate their tribe power!

Pick up. Drop. Strategize. Conquer.

Every single turn transforms the entire board. No two games ever play the same.
This is the hook. Explain Mancala briefly if needed. The key insight is that unlike most worker placement games, the workers are already there. You're redistributing them. This creates an ever-changing puzzle.

The Five Tribes

Each tribe grants a unique power when collected:

🔹

Viziers (Yellow)

Political influence — keep them for powerful end-game majority scoring

🔴

Assassins (Red)

Eliminate rival meeples and claim territory for yourself

🔵

Builders (Blue)

Generate gold coins based on valuable surrounding tiles

🟢

Merchants (Green)

Collect valuable resource cards — sets are worth big points

⚪

Elders (White)

Worth victory points & spent to summon the powerful Djinns

Walk through each tribe. The beauty is that every tribe is useful and you need to balance when to use each one. Viziers are long-term, Assassins are tactical, Builders make money, Merchants collect sets, and Elders summon Djinns.

Summon the Djinns

Invoke Ancient Powers

  • 22 unique Djinn cards with game-changing abilities
  • Spend Elder meeples (white) to summon them
  • Each Djinn grants special powers that bend the rules
  • They also award victory points at game end
  • Choosing which Djinn to pursue can define your entire strategy
"The Djinns are the spice that makes every game feel completely different."
The Djinns are what elevate this game. Some let you score extra for palm trees, some give you extra actions, some protect your meeples. They create asymmetric play and exciting combos.

This is the key selling point for strategy gamers. There's no dominant strategy. You have to read the board state and adapt. One game you might go heavy on Viziers, the next you might focus on Djinns and territory.
